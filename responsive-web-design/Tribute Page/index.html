The journey of Phil Knight

  • 1938 - Born in Portland, Oregon
  • 1959 - Graduated from The University of Oregon with Journalism degree
  • 1959 - served 1 year of active duty in the Army with an additional 7 years in the Army reserve
  • 1962 - Graduated from Stanford Graduate School of Business with a Masters Degree in Business Administration
  • 1962 - Discovered the Tiger-brand running shoes and secured the rights to distribute the shoes in America
  • 1964 - Co-founded Blue Ribbon (the precursor of Nike) with his coach, Bill Bowerman
  • 1971 - Birth of Nike swoosh. Created by Carolyn Davidson with the name suggested by Phil's friend, Jeff Johnson
  • 1980 - Nike went public
  • 2004 - Phil Knight resigned as CEO and passed the title to former CEO of S.C. Johnson & Son, William Perez
  • 2011 - Became 22nd Richest American and 66th richest man in the world with an estimated net worth of $13.1 Billion

“So that morning in 1962 I told myself: Let everyone else call your idea crazy . . . just keep going. Don't stop. Don't even think about stopping until you get there, and don't give much thought to where “there” is. Whatever comes, just don't stop.”

-- Phil Knight, Shoe Dog: A Memoir by the Creator of Nike
